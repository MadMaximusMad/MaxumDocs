# Capital Entry

Capital enters the system through bonding and secondary market acquisition.

Bonding is the system’s primary intake mechanism. Participants exchange external assets for MAX at a discount relative to market conditions. Deposited assets are transferred directly into the treasury, increasing the system’s balance sheet and Risk-Free Value.

In return, participants receive MAX subject to vesting. Vesting enforces temporal distribution of supply, preventing immediate extraction and ensuring that expansion aligns with system growth.

Issuance is constrained by the balance sheet.

Circulating supply does not exceed RFV. Supply does not expand independently of capital intake. Every unit of supply is tied to the system’s underlying value.

Secondary market acquisition provides immediate exposure through decentralized exchange trading. While it does not directly increase treasury reserves, it activates the system’s Engine through transaction activity and fee generation.

Bonding increases system capacity. Trading drives system throughput. Both are required.
